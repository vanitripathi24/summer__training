Apple Inc. is an American multinational technology company headquartered in Cupertino, California, in Silicon Valley, and known for consumer electronics, software and online services. Founded in 1976 as Apple Computer Company by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak and Ronald Wayne, the company was incorporated by Jobs and Wozniak as Apple Computer, Inc. the following year. Its current name was adopted in 2007 as the company expanded its focus from computers to consumer electronics. Apple is one of the Big Tech companies.

History

Apple Computer Company was founded on April 1, 1976, by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne as a partnership.[5][6] The company's first product was the Apple I, a computer designed and hand-built entirely by Wozniak.Apple Computer, Inc. was incorporated in Cupertino, California,[1] on January 3, 1977,[14][15] without Wayne, who had left and sold his share of the company back to Jobs and Wozniak for $800 only twelve days after having co-founded it.[

Products

Mac Main article: Mac (computer) A MacBook Air, in midnight color, on a wooden desk. MacBook Air with M2 chip An thin iMac in blue color on a desk. iMac with M1 chip Mac, which is short for Macintosh, its official name until 1999, is Apple's line of personal computers that use the company's proprietary macOS operating system. Personal computers were Apple's original business line, but as of the end of 2024 they account for only about eight percent of the company's revenue.[203] There are six Mac computer families in production: iMac: Consumer all-in-one desktop computer, introduced in 1998. Mac Mini: Consumer sub-desktop computer, introduced in 2005. MacBook Pro: Professional notebook, introduced in 2006. MacBook Air: Consumer ultra-thin notebook, introduced in 2008. Mac Studio: Professional small form-factor workstation, introduced in 2022. MacBook Neo: Low-cost ultra-thin notebook, introduced in 2026. IPHONE : The iPhone is Apple's line of smartphones, which run the company's iOS operating system. The first iPhone was unveiled by Steve Jobs on January 9, 2007. iPad Main article: iPad The 2020 iPad Pro on display The iPad is Apple's line of tablets, which run the company's iPadOS operating system. The first-generation iPad was announced on January 27, 2010. The iPad is mainly marketed for consuming multimedia, creating art, working on documents, videoconferencing, and playing games.

Branding The original official logo of Apple was used from 1977 to 1999.[213] According to Steve Jobs, the company's name was inspired by his visit to an apple farm while on a fruitarian diet.[214] Apple's first logo, designed by Ron Wayne, depicts Sir Isaac Newton sitting under an apple tree. It was almost immediately replaced by Rob Janoff's "rainbow Apple", the now-familiar rainbow-colored silhouette of an apple with a bite taken out of it

Finances

As of 2024, Apple was the world's fourth-largest personal computer vendor,[328] the largest vendor of tablet computers,[329] and the largest vendor of mobile phones.[330] It is a Big Tech company.[331][332] In its fiscal year ending in September 2011, Apple reported a total of $108 billion in annual revenues—a significant increase from its 2010 revenues of $65 billion—and nearly $82 billion in cash reserves.[333] On March 19, 2012, Apple announced plans for a $2.65-per-share dividend beginning in the fourth quarter of 2012, per approval by its board of directors.[334] The company's worldwide annual revenue in 2013 totaled $170 billion.[335] In May 2013, Apple entered the top ten of the Fortune 500 list of companies for the first time, rising 11 places above its 2012 ranking to take the sixth position.[336] As of 2016, Apple has around US$234 billion of cash and marketable securities, of which 90% is located outside the United States for tax purposes.[

Environment

Apple Energy Apple Energy, LLC is a wholly owned subsidiary of Apple that sells solar energy. As of June 6, 2016, Apple's solar farms in California and Nevada have been declared to provide 217.9 megawatts of solar generation capacity.[377] Apple has received regulatory approval to construct a landfill gas energy plant in North Carolina to use the methane emissions to generate electricity.[378] Apple's North Carolina data center is already powered entirely by renewable sources.

Supply Chain

Main article: Apple supply chain See also: List of Apple Inc. suppliers As of 2018, Apple used hardware components from 43 different countries.[412] Apple products were made in the United States in Apple-owned factories until the late 1990s; however, as a result of outsourcing initiatives in the 2000s, almost all of its manufacturing is now handled abroad. According to a report by The New York Times, Apple insiders "believe the vast scale of overseas factories, as well as the flexibility, diligence and industrial skills of foreign workers, have so outpaced their American counterparts that 'Made in the U.S.A.' is no longer a viable option for most Apple products".[413] South Korean firms, including Samsung and LG, worked closely with Apple as parts partners for the iMac, iPod, and iPhone before it increased its reliance on Taiwan and China.[414]: 74–232  Taiwanese Apple suppliers such as Foxconn, Wistron, Pegatron, Quanta, and Compal Electronics established a sizable manufacturing presence in China.[415] By the 2020s, orders were shifting to Chinese firms such as to Luxshare, BYD Electronic, Goertek, and Wingtech. By 2021, Apple had more suppliers from China than Taiwan.[414]: 337  In March 2017, The Wall Street Journal reported that Apple would begin manufacturing iPhone models in India "over the next two months",[416] and in May, the Journal wrote that an Apple manufacturer had begun production of iPhone SE in the country,[417] In May 2017, the company announced a $1-billion funding project for "advanced manufacturing" in the United States,[418][419] In April 2019, Apple initiated manufacturing of iPhone 7 at its Bengaluru facility.
